        <section>
            <p>Capitalism, at its core, is an economic system where private individuals and businesses own the means of production, such as factories, land, and resources. It's a way of organizing society to encourage innovation, efficiency, and competition.</p>
            <p>But don't worry if this sounds like gibberish – we'll break it down in simple terms so you can understand it better!</p>
        </section>
        <h2 id="what-are-the-key-features-of-capitalism">What are the key features of capitalism?</h2>
        <ul class="list-disc pl-4 mb-6">
            <li><strong>Private ownership:</strong> Individuals and businesses own the means of production, such as factories, land, and resources.</li>
            <li><strong>Profit motive:</strong> The primary goal is to make a profit by producing goods or services that people want to buy.</li>
            <li><strong>Market forces:</strong> Prices and supply are determined by the market, rather than by government intervention.</li>
            <li><strong>Free enterprise:</strong> Anyone can start their own business and compete with others in the market.</li>
        </ul>
        <h2 id="examples-of-capitalism-in-action">Examples of capitalism in action</h2>
        <section class="bg-white p-4 mb-8 rounded shadow-md">
            <h3 class="text-lg font-bold mb-2">Example 1: A coffee shop owner</h3>
            <p>Imagine a small business owner who opens a coffee shop. They invest their own money, borrow some from the bank, and work hard to create a cozy atmosphere and delicious coffee. Their goal is to make a profit by selling coffee to customers.</p>
        </section>
        <section class="bg-white p-4 mb-8 rounded shadow-md">
            <h3 class="text-lg font-bold mb-2">Example 2: A multinational corporation</h3>
            <p>Now imagine a giant company like Apple or Google, which operates globally and employs thousands of people. They design, manufacture, and market products that customers love – all in pursuit of making a profit.</p>
        </section>
        <h2 id="criticisms-of-capitalism">Criticisms of capitalism</h2>
        <ul class="list-disc pl-4 mb-6">
            <li><strong>Income inequality:</strong> Some people accumulate vast wealth, while others struggle to make ends meet.</li>
            <li><strong>Exploitation:</strong> Workers may be underpaid or overworked in the pursuit of profit.</li>
            <li><strong>Environmental degradation:</strong> Companies might prioritize short-term gains over long-term sustainability.</li>
        </ul>
        <p>While capitalism has lifted millions out of poverty and created unprecedented prosperity, it's not without its flaws. Critics argue that it can lead to social and environmental problems if left unchecked.</p>
