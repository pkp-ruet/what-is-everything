    <h1 class="text-3xl font-bold mb-4">What is Electricity?</h1>
  </header>

  <section>
    <p>Electricity is a fundamental form of energy that has revolutionized the way we live and work. It is a type of energy that is generated from various sources such as coal, gas, nuclear power, solar energy, and wind energy.</p>

    <ul class="list-disc pl-5 mb-4">
      <li>Electricity is a form of kinetic energy, which is the energy of motion.</li>
      <li>It can be generated, transmitted, and distributed to various devices and appliances for use.</li>
      <li>Electricity is measured in units of watts (W), kilowatts (kW), or megawatts (MW).</li>
    </ul>

    <p>The concept of electricity dates back to ancient times when people used static electricity from rubbing amber against fur to attract small objects. However, the modern understanding and harnessing of electricity began in the 19th century with the work of scientists such as Benjamin Franklin, Alessandro Volta, and Michael Faraday.</p>

    <ol class="list-decimal pl-5 mb-4">
      <li>Benjamin Franklin conducted extensive research on electricity, demonstrating its connection to lightning and developing a theory of positive and negative charges.</li>
      <li>Alessandro Volta invented the first battery, known as the voltaic pile, which consisted of stacked discs of copper and zinc separated by cardboard soaked in saltwater.</li>
      <li>Michael Faraday discovered the principles of electromagnetic induction, which led to the development of generators, motors, and transformers.</li>
    </ol>

    <p>Today, electricity is a vital part of modern life, powering everything from homes and businesses to transportation systems and communication networks. It has enabled countless technological advancements and transformed the way we live, work, and interact with each other.</p>

    <section>
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-4">Real-World Applications</h2>

      <ul class="list-disc pl-5 mb-4">
        <li>Electricity powers homes, businesses, and industries, providing lighting, heating, cooling, and energy for various appliances.</li>
        <li>It drives transportation systems, including electric vehicles, trains, and aircraft.</li>
        <li>Communication networks rely on electricity to transmit data, voice, and video signals over long distances.</li>
      </ul>

      <p>The importance of electricity cannot be overstated. It has transformed the way we live, work, and interact with each other. As technology continues to advance, it is likely that electricity will play an increasingly vital role in shaping our future.</p>
