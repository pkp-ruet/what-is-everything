    <section class="mb-6">
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-gray-900 mb-2">🧠 Introduction</h2>
      <p>
        In today’s cloud-native world, managing applications across multiple servers can quickly become complex.
        Enter <strong>Kubernetes</strong> — an open-source container orchestration platform that automates deployment,
        scaling, and management of containerized applications.
      </p>
    </section>

    <section class="mb-6">
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-gray-900 mb-2">🚢 What are Containers?</h2>
      <p>
        Before diving into Kubernetes, let’s briefly understand <strong>containers</strong>. Containers are lightweight,
        portable units that package code, dependencies, and runtime together — ensuring consistency across environments
        from development to production.
      </p>
    </section>

    <section class="mb-6">
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-gray-900 mb-2">🔧 So, What is Kubernetes?</h2>
      <p>
        Kubernetes (often abbreviated as <strong>K8s</strong>) is a platform developed by Google, now maintained by the
        <strong>Cloud Native Computing Foundation (CNCF)</strong>. It acts like a control system for your containerized applications.
      </p>
      <p class="mt-2">
        Kubernetes handles:
      </p>
      <ul class="list-disc list-inside ml-4 mt-2 text-gray-700">
        <li><strong>Deployment</strong> of applications</li>
        <li><strong>Scaling</strong> apps up or down</li>
        <li><strong>Load balancing</strong></li>
        <li><strong>Self-healing</strong> of failed containers</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section class="mb-6">
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-gray-900 mb-2">⚙️ Core Components</h2>
      <p>
        Kubernetes includes several key components:
      </p>
      <ul class="list-disc list-inside ml-4 mt-2 text-gray-700">
        <li><strong>Pod:</strong> The smallest deployable unit that can hold one or more containers.</li>
        <li><strong>Node:</strong> A physical or virtual machine running pods.</li>
        <li><strong>Cluster:</strong> A group of nodes managed by Kubernetes.</li>
        <li><strong>Control Plane:</strong> Manages the state and configuration of the cluster.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section class="mb-6">
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-gray-900 mb-2">🌐 Why Use Kubernetes?</h2>
      <p>
        Kubernetes has become the industry standard because it simplifies the deployment and scaling of applications.
        It supports all major cloud providers, hybrid environments, and even on-premise setups.
      </p>
    </section>

    <section class="mb-6">
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-gray-900 mb-2">🚀 Conclusion</h2>
      <p>
        Kubernetes empowers developers to build and manage modern applications with speed, flexibility, and confidence.
        As companies scale their infrastructure, Kubernetes becomes a vital part of their DevOps toolkit.
      </p>
    </section>
