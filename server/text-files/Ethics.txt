    </header>
    <section class="markdown">
        <p>At its core, ethics is a set of principles that guide an individual's or organization's behavior in regards to what is right and wrong. It's a framework for making decisions that takes into account the impact on others, the environment, and society as a whole.</p>
        
        <h2 class="text-lg font-bold mb-4">What are Ethics?</h2>
        <ul>
            <li>Ethics is concerned with what is morally right or wrong</li>
            <li>It involves making decisions based on principles that promote the well-being of others and society</li>
            <li>Ethics is a universal concept, applicable across cultures and time</li>
        </ul>

        <h2 class="text-lg font-bold mb-4">Key Principles of Ethics</h2>
        <ol>
            <li>Honesty: being truthful in all interactions and transactions</li>
            <li>Respect: valuing the dignity, autonomy, and rights of others</li>
            <li>Fairness: ensuring that individuals are treated justly and without bias</li>
            <li>Responsibility: taking ownership of one's actions and their consequences</li>
        </ol>

        <h2 class="text-lg font-bold mb-4">Real-World Applications of Ethics</h2>
        <p>Ethics plays a crucial role in various aspects of life, including business, healthcare, education, and government.</p>
        
        <p>For instance:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>In the business world, ethics is essential for maintaining trust with customers and stakeholders. Companies must adhere to principles such as transparency, accountability, and social responsibility</li>
            <li>In healthcare, ethics guides medical professionals in making decisions that balance individual patient needs with broader societal concerns.</li>
        </ul>

        <h2 class="text-lg font-bold mb-4">Conclusion</h2>
        <p>Ethics provides a foundation for moral decision-making, guiding individuals and organizations to act responsibly and with integrity. By understanding and applying key principles of ethics, we can create a more just, equitable, and compassionate society.</p>
