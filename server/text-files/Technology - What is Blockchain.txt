    <h1>What is Blockchain? The Digital Ledger That Doesn’t Lie</h1>

    <p>Imagine a notebook that everyone can read, no one can erase, and everyone trusts. That’s the basic idea behind <span class="highlight">blockchain</span> — a revolutionary way to record and share data that’s changing how the world handles trust.</p>

    <p>At its core, a blockchain is a <strong>chain of digital “blocks”</strong> — each block contains data (like a transaction), a timestamp, and a special fingerprint called a <em>hash</em>. Once a block is added to the chain, it’s linked to the one before it — forming a secure, tamper-proof timeline of information.</p>

    <p>Unlike traditional systems where one company controls the database, a blockchain is <span class="highlight">decentralized</span>. That means it’s shared across many computers (called nodes), and everyone has a copy. If someone tries to mess with the data, the others won’t agree — and the cheat won’t work.</p>

    <p>This makes blockchain perfect for things where <strong>trust</strong> and <strong>security</strong> matter:</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Digital currencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum</li>
      <li>Secure voting systems</li>
      <li>Transparent supply chains</li>
      <li>Smart contracts and digital ownership</li>
    </ul>

    <p>It’s not magic. It’s math, computers, and clever engineering. But it’s changing the way we think about ownership, honesty, and digital freedom.</p>
