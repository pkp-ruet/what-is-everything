    <h1>What is Probability? The Math Behind Maybe</h1>

    <p>Ever flipped a coin and guessed heads? That tiny moment — of chance, choice, and uncertainty — is all about <span class="highlight">probability</span>.</p>

    <p><strong>Probability</strong> is the branch of math that helps us understand the <em>likelihood</em> of something happening. It doesn’t predict the future — it tells us how likely different outcomes are.</p>

    <p>Here’s the basic idea:</p>
    <ul>
      <li>If something is <strong>certain</strong>, its probability is <strong>1</strong> (or 100%).</li>
      <li>If something is <strong>impossible</strong>, its probability is <strong>0</strong>.</li>
      <li>Most things in life? Somewhere in between.</li>
    </ul>

    <p>Let’s take a classic example:<br>
    🎲 Roll a fair 6-sided die. What’s the chance you get a 3?<br>
    There’s only <strong>one 3</strong>, and <strong>six</strong> total possibilities.<br>
    So, <strong>Probability = 1 / 6 ≈ 16.7%</strong></p>

    <p>Simple! But probability isn’t just for games — it powers weather forecasts, medical tests, AI, finance, and even your favorite streaming suggestions.</p>

    <p>Probability helps us <strong>make better decisions</strong> in an uncertain world. It’s not magic — it’s just math for managing the unknown.</p>
