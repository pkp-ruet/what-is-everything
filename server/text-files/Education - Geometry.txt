    <h1>What is Geometry? The Shape of the World Around You</h1>

    <p>Look around you — the screen you’re reading, the window beside you, even your pizza slice — it’s all <span class="highlight">geometry</span> in action.</p>

    <p><strong>Geometry</strong> is the branch of math that deals with <em>shapes, sizes, angles, and space</em>. It helps us understand how things fit, move, and relate in the physical world.</p>

    <p>The word <strong>"geometry"</strong> comes from ancient Greek — meaning “earth measurement.” And that’s exactly what it was: a way to measure land, build pyramids, and understand the stars.</p>

    <p>In geometry, we study:</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Points</strong> – the tiniest, most precise spots</li>
      <li><strong>Lines & Angles</strong> – how space stretches and turns</li>
      <li><strong>Shapes</strong> – from triangles and circles to polygons and 3D solids</li>
      <li><strong>Area & Volume</strong> – how much space something covers or holds</li>
    </ul>

    <p>Geometry isn’t just for architects or artists — it’s everywhere:</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Engineers use it to design bridges.</li>
      <li>Game designers use it to create worlds.</li>
      <li>Nature uses it to form snowflakes, honeycombs, and shells.</li>
    </ul>

    <p>When you learn geometry, you’re not just learning math — you’re learning how to <strong>see</strong> the world differently.</p>
